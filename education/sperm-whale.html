Sperm Whale

The Deep-Diving Giant of the Ocean

The sperm whale is the largest toothed predator on Earth. It can dive over 2,000 meters deep to hunt giant squid in the ocean’s darkest zones. These whales have the biggest brain of any animal and use powerful echolocation to navigate and find prey underwater.

Quick Facts

Common Name: Sperm Whale

Scientific Name: Physeter macrocephalus

Type: Mammal

Diet: Carnivore (mainly squid)

Average Lifespan: 60 – 70 years

Size: Up to 20 meters (66 feet)

Weight: Up to 57,000 kg (125,000 lbs)

Sperm whales live in pods and can hold their breath for over an hour. Their block-shaped heads contain a special organ called a "spermaceti organ," which may help in buoyancy and sonar-like sound generation. Though once heavily hunted for oil, sperm whales are now protected, yet still affected by noise pollution and plastic debris.
