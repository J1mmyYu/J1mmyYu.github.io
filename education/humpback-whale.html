Humpback Whale

The Singing Travelers of the Sea

Humpback whales are known for their beautiful and complex songs, often sung by males during mating season. They are famous for leaping out of the water and migrating thousands of kilometers each year between feeding and breeding areas.

Quick Facts

Common Name: Humpback Whale

Scientific Name: Megaptera novaeangliae

Type: Mammal

Diet: Krill and small fish

Average Lifespan: 45 – 50 years

Size: 12 to 16 meters (39–52 feet)

Weight: 25,000–30,000 kg (55,000–66,000 lbs)

Conservation efforts have helped humpback whale populations recover in recent decades after commercial whaling. Today, they remain protected and continue to inspire awe with their songs and migrations.
