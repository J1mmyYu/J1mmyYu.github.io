Polar Bear

The King of the Arctic

Polar bears are powerful predators perfectly adapted to life on the Arctic sea ice. They are excellent swimmers and rely on sea ice to hunt their main prey: seals. Their thick fur and a layer of fat help them survive in freezing temperatures.

Quick Facts

Common Name: Polar Bear

Scientific Name: Ursus maritimus

Type: Mammal

Diet: Carnivore (mainly seals)

Average Lifespan: 20 – 25 years

Size: 2.4 to 3 meters (8–10 feet)

Weight: 350–700 kg (770–1,540 lbs)

Climate change is reducing the amount of sea ice, making it harder for polar bears to find food. Conservation efforts focus on reducing emissions and protecting critical habitats to ensure the future of this majestic Arctic animal.
