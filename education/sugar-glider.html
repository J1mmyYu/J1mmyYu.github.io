Sugar Glider

The Flying Possum of the Night

Sugar gliders are small, nocturnal marsupials from Australia and Indonesia. With flaps of skin between their limbs, they can glide between trees up to 50 meters apart!

Quick Facts

Common Name: Sugar Glider

Scientific Name: Petaurus breviceps

Type: Marsupial

Diet: Omnivore (sap, nectar, insects)

Lifespan: 10–12 years (in captivity)

Size: 24–30 cm (including tail)

Weight: 90–130 grams

Sugar gliders live in family groups and use chirps, squeaks, and even purring to communicate. Their large eyes help them see in the dark as they leap through forest canopies!
