Buck

The Antlered Athlete of the Woods

A "buck" is a mature male deer, recognized by its impressive antlers. During mating season, bucks use their antlers to challenge rivals and attract mates.

Quick Facts

Common Name: Buck

Scientific Name: Varies by species (e.g., Odocoileus virginianus)

Type: Mammal

Diet: Herbivore (leaves, grasses, twigs)

Lifespan: 6–14 years

Height: 90–105 cm (at shoulder)

Weight: 60–150 kg (130–330 lbs)

Bucks are found worldwide, from forests to grasslands. Their antlers shed and regrow annually, often growing larger each year.
