Kingfisher

The Colorful, Fast-Diving Bird

Kingfishers are vibrant birds known for their incredible speed and precision when diving to catch fish. Their specialized eyes allow them to judge underwater distance and depth perfectly.

Quick Facts

Common Name: Kingfisher

Scientific Name: Alcedo atthis

Type: Bird

Diet: Carnivore (mainly fish)

Lifespan: Up to 15 years

Size: 16–18 cm (6.3–7.1 in)

Weight: About 35 grams (1.2 oz)

They typically nest near rivers and lakes, digging burrows into sandy banks. Their colors shine vividly in sunlight, making them favorites among birdwatchers.
