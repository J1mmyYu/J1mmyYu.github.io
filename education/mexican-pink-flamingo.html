Mexican Pink Flamingo

The Dancer of the Wetlands

The vivid pink color of flamingos comes from their shrimp-rich diet. These birds are known for standing on one leg and performing stunning synchronized dances during mating season.

Quick Facts

Common Name: Mexican Pink Flamingo

Scientific Name: Phoenicopterus ruber

Type: Bird

Diet: Omnivore (algae, crustaceans)

Lifespan: Up to 40 years

Height: 120–145 cm (4.7 ft)

Weight: 2–4 kg (4.4–8.8 lbs)

Found in the Yucatán Peninsula and Caribbean, flamingos nest in large colonies. Their beaks are specially adapted to filter food from the water.
